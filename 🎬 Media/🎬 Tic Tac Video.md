# Tic Tac Video

## Overview

The Tic Tac Video, officially known as the [[ğŸ¬ FLIR1 Video]], is one of the most significant pieces of UAP evidence released by the U.S. government. The name "Tic Tac" refers to the object's white, oblong appearance similar to the popular breath mint. This footage was captured during the [[ğŸ“œ USS Nimitz Incident]] in 2004 and was officially released by the Pentagon in 2017.

## Technical Details

### Recording Information
- **Date**: November 14, 2004
- **Location**: Pacific Ocean, ~100 miles off San Diego coast
- **Platform**: F/A-18F Super Hornet
- **Sensor**: Advanced Targeting Forward Looking Infrared (ATFLIR) Pod
- **Duration**: ~76 seconds
- **Pilot**: [[ğŸ‘¤ Chad Underwood]]
- **Squadron**: VFA-41 Black Aces

### Object Characteristics
- Oblong/"Tic Tac" shape
- Approximately 40 feet in length
- White/off-white coloration
- No visible exhaust plumes
- No conventional flight surfaces
- No visible means of propulsion
