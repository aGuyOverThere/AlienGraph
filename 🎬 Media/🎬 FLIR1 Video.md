# FLIR1 Video (Tic Tac)

## Overview

The FLIR1 video, also known as the "Tic Tac" video, is one of the most significant pieces of UAP evidence ever released by the U.S. government. Captured in 2004 by [[ğŸ‘¤ Chad Underwood]] during the [[ğŸ“œ USS Nimitz Incident]], the video shows an unidentified object displaying extraordinary flight characteristics. The footage was officially released by the Pentagon in 2017 and has been authenticated by multiple government agencies.

- **Location**: Pacific Ocean, ~100 miles off San Diego coast
- **Platform**: F/A-18F Super Hornet
- **Sensor**: Advanced Targeting Forward Looking Infrared (ATFLIR) Pod
- **Duration**: ~76 seconds
- **Pilot**: [[ğŸ‘¤ Chad Underwood]]

## Technical Analysis

### Flight Characteristics
- Rapid acceleration
- Instantaneous heading changes
- No visible flight control surfaces
- Trans-medium travel capability
- No sonic boom despite supersonic movement
- No thermal signature consistent with propulsion

### Sensor Data
- Multiple radar systems tracked object
- FLIR camera locked onto target
- Clear infrared signature
- Multiple sensor confirmations
- No conventional explanations identified
